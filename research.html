Advancing environmental health through interdisciplinary research in WASH, infectious disease control, and health systems strengthening.

Research Experience

CIHR Health Systems Impact Fellow

BC Ministry of Health, Sciences and Guidelines Development (Sep 2024 - Aug 2025) [cite: 22]

Focus: Investigating failure risks and health hazards in BC's water distribution systems to inform policy and best practices[cite: 23].

Graduate Research Assistant

Environmental Systems Engineering, University of British Columbia (Sep 2021 - Present) [cite: 15]

Supervisor: Prof. Sara E. Beck. Research on virucidal effects of disinfectants/UV/sunlight on biofilms[cite: 15], microbial community analysis[cite: 16], biofilm characterization (CLSM, ATP)[cite: 17], and Mpox virus inactivation studies informing WHO policy[cite: 18, 19, 20, 21].

Postdoctoral Researcher

Civil and Environmental Health Dept., Tufts University (Sep 2018 - Jun 2019) [cite: 24]

Supervisor: Prof Daniele Lantagne. Led research on CISUR interventions in emergencies[cite: 24], evaluated Goma UFPSE water program (cholera response)[cite: 25], analyzed Haiti WASH programs[cite: 26], studied membrane filter fouling[cite: 27], and collaborated on novel ceramic water filter development (Kohler partnership)[cite: 28, 29].

Co-Investigator, Baby WASH project

Johns Hopkins University School of Public Health / Food for the Hungry (Aug 2017 - Sep 2018) [cite: 29]

PI: Prof Christine M George. Investigated links between environmental enteropathies and child development in DRC[cite: 29]. Coordinated implementation, training, data management, and dissemination[cite: 31, 32]. Resulted in 12 peer-reviewed articles[cite: 32].
