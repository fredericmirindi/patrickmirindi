About Me

With over a decade of experience spanning clinical practice, academic research, teaching, and global health consultancy, I bridge the gap between scientific inquiry and real-world application. My PhD research at the University of British Columbia investigates innovative solutions for water sanitation and infectious disease control, particularly focusing on surface disinfection and biofilm analysis.

I have a proven track record of leading international collaborations (e.g., WHO, Johns Hopkins, Tufts), developing impactful training programs, and contributing to policy through evidence-based research. Fluent in multiple languages including English, French, and Swahili, I excel in diverse, multicultural settings.
